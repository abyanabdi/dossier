CASE FILE 002 / FINAL ASSESSMENT

The Executive Impersonation Incident

Complete the investigator assessment. You must demonstrate your understanding of the digital evidence collected throughout the case.

Assessment Rules

  • 10 multiple-choice questions
  • 90% required to successfully close the investigation
  • 80% requires evidence review and reassessment
  • Below 80% requires additional investigation

A) Malware installation
B) Social engineering and impersonation
C) Physical device theft
D) Network destruction

A) It automatically transferred money
B) It bypassed all security systems

B) The email was received during working hours
C) The message contained a signature

B) Suitable target
C) Criminal organization
D) Guardian

5. Why did urgency make the attack more successful?

B) It prevented digital evidence collection

D) It changed the transaction amount

6. What made the financial transaction suspicious?

A) The payment was made electronically
B) The transaction occurred in the morning
C) The employee used a computer
D) The recipient account was new and lacked transaction history

7. What role did the employee play in the attack?

A) They intentionally committed the crime
B) They were manipulated into unknowingly assisting the fraud
C) They created the AI voice
D) They controlled the attacker

8. Why is this considered a digital investigation case?

A) Investigators analyzed digital evidence such as emails, audio, and transaction records
B) The crime only involved physical evidence
C) No technology was involved
D) Only financial records mattered

9. What security improvement would best prevent this type of attack?

A) Allowing faster payments
B) Removing employee access
C) Requiring independent verification before large transfers
D) Ignoring suspicious communication

10. What is the main lesson from this investigation?

A) Technology is the only cause of cybercrime
B) Employees are always responsible for fraud
C) Cybercrime often combines technology, human behaviour, and opportunity
D) Financial crimes cannot be prevented
