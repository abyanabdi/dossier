ABOUT DOSSIER

Reimagining How We Learn Investigations

Dossier is an interactive investigation platform that transforms digital crime, forensic science, and cybersecurity concepts into realistic case-based learning experiences.

What is Dossier?

Dossier is a digital investigation education platform designed to allow users to experience the investigative process firsthand.

Instead of only learning about cybercrime through definitions and theories, Dossier places users inside simulated investigations where they must review evidence, identify patterns, question information, and determine what conclusions are supported by the available facts.

Each investigation is designed around the idea that real investigations are built through observation, analysis, and careful decision-making.

By combining realistic case scenarios with evidence analysis and assessments, Dossier creates a learning environment where users can explore how modern investigators approach digital threats.

Why Dossier Exists

The way crime happens is changing.

Technology has transformed how people communicate, work, and interact online. As a result, evidence that was once found mainly in physical locations can now exist across emails, websites, online accounts, digital footprints, and artificial intelligence systems.

Understanding these forms of evidence has become an important part of modern investigations. However, many people learn about cybersecurity and digital forensics through technical explanations that can feel disconnected from real situations.

Dossier was created to bridge that gap by turning complex investigation concepts into interactive experiences where users can explore problems the way investigators do.

Our Mission

Dossier's mission is to make investigation education more engaging, accessible, and connected to the challenges of the modern digital world.

The platform focuses on developing investigative thinking rather than simply providing information. Users learn how to analyze evidence, recognize patterns, evaluate credibility, and make decisions based on facts.

Through interactive cases, research materials, and evidence-based challenges, Dossier aims to help students understand the relationship between technology, crime, and investigation.

The Dossier Experience

01 / INVESTIGATE

Explore Realistic Cases

Users enter simulated investigations involving cybercrime, fraud, online deception, and emerging digital threats.

Each case presents a challenge where the user must think critically and determine what happened.

02 / ANALYZE

Review Digital Evidence

Users examine evidence such as emails, websites, digital footprints, and online activity.

Small details can reveal important information about a case.

03 / CONCLUDE

Make Evidence-Based Decisions

Users complete assessments and determine case outcomes based on the evidence they discovered.

The goal is to develop reasoning skills used in real investigations.

The Investigator Mindset

Every investigation begins with asking the right questions.

  • What evidence supports this claim?
  • Can the information be verified?
  • Are there suspicious patterns or inconsistencies?
  • What alternative explanations should be considered?
  • How could the incident have been prevented?

Dossier encourages users to think beyond simply finding answers. Investigators must understand evidence, challenge assumptions, and make decisions based on reliable information.
