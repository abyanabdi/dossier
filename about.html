OUR PURPOSE

Teach people how to think like investigators.

Dossier combines criminology, forensic science, cybercrime, digital evidence, and investigative reasoning in one interactive platform. Each case requires users to identify what matters, separate fact from assumption, evaluate competing explanations, and reach a conclusion supported by evidence.

The goal is not simply to reveal an answer. The goal is to build the judgment, patience, curiosity, and analytical discipline required to understand how reliable investigations are conducted.

Immersive Case Simulations Investigations unfold through realistic evidence, decisions, interviews, and forensic findings.
Evidence-Based Reasoning Conclusions must be supported by verified information rather than instinct or first impressions.
Practical Skill Development Users practice digital verification, evidence comparison, timeline analysis, and investigative judgment.
Responsible Investigation The platform emphasizes limitations, uncertainty, ethics, and the danger of unsupported assumptions.

CORE PRINCIPLES

What guides the platform

Every Dossier investigation is designed around principles that support careful, fair, and defensible investigative reasoning.

⌕
01

Examine Before Concluding

An object, message, record, or behaviour may appear suspicious without proving anything. Investigators must determine what the evidence actually establishes before forming a conclusion.

◇
02

Separate Fact From Assumption

Observed information must remain distinct from interpretation. The platform encourages users to recognize when they are relying on evidence and when they are making an inference.

◎
03

Question Initial Impressions

Confidence, nervousness, authority, familiarity, and professional appearance can all be misleading. First impressions should guide further inquiry, not determine guilt or credibility.

▦
04

Compare Independent Sources

Strong findings emerge when physical evidence, digital records, timelines, statements, and laboratory results support the same explanation.

△
05

Test Alternative Explanations

A persuasive theory must explain the evidence better than competing possibilities. Investigators should actively look for information that could challenge their preferred conclusion.

✓
06

Report Uncertainty Honestly

Responsible analysis explains what is known, what remains uncertain, what information is missing, and what the available evidence cannot prove.

LEARNING MODEL

How users move through an investigation

Dossier investigations follow a structured learning process that requires active participation at every stage.

▤

Receive the Briefing

Review the incident, known facts, persons involved, initial evidence, and investigative objective.

⌕

Examine the Evidence

Inspect documents, digital records, physical objects, laboratory findings, and environmental details.

◎

Question and Compare

Interview relevant individuals, identify contradictions, and compare statements with independent records.

◇

Build a Case Theory

Organize means, motive, opportunity, timing, access, and corroborating evidence into a defensible explanation.

✓

Defend the Conclusion

Complete the assessment and explain which evidence supports the final investigative decision.
