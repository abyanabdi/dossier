ABOUT DOSSIER

The Future of Investigation Education

Dossier is an interactive investigation platform designed to explore digital evidence, cybercrime, forensic science, and emerging technologies through realistic case-based learning.

What is Dossier?

Dossier is an interactive digital investigation education platform that allows users to experience the process of analyzing evidence, investigating cybercrime, and developing investigative reasoning.

Rather than learning through definitions alone, Dossier places users inside realistic investigation scenarios where they must examine evidence, identify suspicious patterns, and determine what conclusions are supported by the available information.

The platform combines criminology, forensic science, cybersecurity, and emerging technologies to explore how investigations are conducted in a world where crime increasingly exists online.

Through case files, evidence reviews, assessments, and research resources, Dossier transforms complex investigation concepts into engaging learning experiences.

Why Dossier Was Created

The nature of crime is changing.

Traditional investigations have often focused on physical evidence such as fingerprints, documents, and crime scenes. While these forms of evidence remain important, modern investigations now depend heavily on digital information.

Emails, websites, online accounts, social media activity, metadata, artificial intelligence, and digital communication can all become important sources of evidence.

However, many people encounter these topics only through technical explanations that can feel complex or disconnected from real-world situations.

Dossier was created to make investigation education more accessible by allowing users to experience the process of investigating digital threats instead of only reading about them.

What Problem Does Dossier Solve?

Cybercrime and digital investigations are becoming increasingly important, yet many educational resources do not show how these concepts work in practice.

Students may learn about cybercrime, digital forensics, and criminal behaviour separately without understanding how these areas connect during an actual investigation.

Dossier bridges the gap between theory and practice by creating interactive experiences that allow users to:

  • Analyze realistic digital evidence
  • Recognize cybercrime warning signs
  • Understand criminal methods and behaviours
  • Evaluate information and identify inconsistencies
  • Develop evidence-based conclusions

The goal is to make investigation education more practical, engaging, and connected to the challenges of the modern digital world.

Our Mission

Dossier's mission is to make modern investigation education more accessible, interactive, and engaging for students and future investigators.

The platform aims to connect academic concepts with real-world applications by showing how ideas from criminology, forensic science, cybersecurity, and technology work together.

Dossier encourages users to develop an investigator mindset by learning how to question information, analyze evidence, recognize patterns, and make decisions based on facts.

The objective is not simply to provide answers, but to teach users how investigations are approached and how conclusions are reached.

The Dossier Experience

STEP 01

Investigate Cases

Users enter realistic investigation scenarios involving cybercrime, fraud, digital identity threats, and online deception.

Each case challenges users to examine evidence and think like an investigator.

STEP 02

Analyze Evidence

Users review different forms of evidence including emails, websites, digital footprints, and online activity.

The goal is to identify patterns, warning signs, and important investigative details.

STEP 03

Complete Investigations

Users complete assessments that test their ability to interpret evidence and determine case outcomes.

Investigations are evaluated through scoring, feedback, and investigator rankings.

What Dossier Explores

Digital Forensics

Exploring how investigators collect, preserve, and interpret digital evidence from devices, accounts, and online environments.

Cybercrime Investigation

Examining online fraud, phishing, social engineering, identity theft, and the methods criminals use in digital spaces.

Artificial Intelligence

Understanding how AI is changing both criminal activity and the tools investigators use to detect and analyze threats.

Human Behaviour and Crime

Exploring how trust, decision-making, manipulation, and psychology influence digital crime.

The Investigator Mindset

Every investigation begins with asking the right questions.

  • What evidence supports this conclusion?
  • Can the information be verified?
  • Are there patterns or inconsistencies?
  • What alternative explanations should be considered?
  • How could this incident have been prevented?

Dossier focuses on developing critical thinking skills because successful investigations depend on careful analysis, curiosity, and attention to detail.
