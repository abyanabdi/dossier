ABOUT DOSSIER

Reimagining How We Learn Investigations

Dossier is an interactive investigation platform that transforms digital investigations, cybercrime, forensic science, and artificial intelligence into realistic case-based learning experiences.

What is Dossier?

Dossier is an educational platform where users learn by investigating.

Instead of only reading about cybercrime or digital evidence, users work through simulated investigations that require them to review evidence, identify suspicious patterns, evaluate information, and determine what conclusions are supported by the available facts.

Every investigation is designed around the idea that effective investigators rely on careful observation, critical thinking, and evidence-based decision making. By combining realistic case scenarios with interactive evidence and assessments, Dossier creates an engaging environment where users can experience how modern digital investigations are conducted.

Why Dossier Exists

Technology has transformed the way people communicate, work, and interact online. As a result, crime has evolved beyond physical spaces, creating new forms of digital evidence that investigators must understand and analyze.

Today, important evidence can exist within emails, websites, online accounts, digital footprints, social media activity, and artificial intelligence systems. Understanding these sources of information has become an essential part of modern investigations.

Dossier was created to make these concepts easier to understand by transforming them into interactive learning experiences that encourage users to think through problems the way investigators do.

Our Mission

Dossier's mission is to make investigation education more engaging, accessible, and relevant to today's digital world.

The platform focuses on developing investigative thinking by encouraging users to analyze evidence, recognize patterns, evaluate credibility, question assumptions, and make decisions supported by facts.

Through interactive case files, research resources, and evidence-based learning, Dossier aims to help users better understand the relationship between technology, crime, and modern investigations.

The Dossier Experience

01 / INVESTIGATE

Explore Realistic Cases

Every investigation begins with a realistic digital case involving cybercrime, online fraud, social engineering, or emerging technologies. Users review the available information and begin building an understanding of what happened.

02 / ANALYZE

Review Digital Evidence

Investigators examine emails, websites, digital footprints, online activity, and other evidence to identify suspicious indicators and connect important details throughout the investigation.

03 / CONCLUDE

Make Evidence-Based Decisions

After reviewing the available evidence, users complete an investigation assessment and determine the outcome of the case using the facts they have collected.

What Dossier Explores

Digital Forensics

Learn how investigators collect, preserve, analyze, and interpret digital evidence from computers, mobile devices, online accounts, and internet activity.

Cybercrime Investigation

Explore phishing attacks, identity theft, online fraud, social engineering, financial scams, and other forms of cyber-enabled crime.

Artificial Intelligence

Understand how artificial intelligence is changing both criminal behaviour and modern investigative techniques while creating new opportunities and new challenges.

Human Behaviour & Crime

Examine how trust, manipulation, psychology, and decision making influence online behaviour and play an important role in digital crime.

The Investigator Mindset

Every investigation begins with asking the right questions.

  • What evidence supports this claim?
  • Can the information be independently verified?
  • Are there inconsistencies or suspicious patterns?
  • What alternative explanations should be considered?
  • How could this incident have been prevented?

Dossier encourages users to think beyond simply finding answers. Effective investigations require curiosity, careful observation, and the ability to evaluate evidence before reaching conclusions.
