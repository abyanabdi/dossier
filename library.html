EVIDENCE WORKFLOW

The investigative evidence process

Reliable evidence analysis follows a documented process. Skipping a stage can weaken the reliability of the evidence and the conclusion built from it.

⌕

Identify

Recognize potentially relevant objects, records, statements, systems, and environmental conditions.

▣

Preserve

Protect the original condition of the evidence and prevent loss, alteration, or contamination.

⚗

Examine

Use appropriate investigative or scientific methods to determine what information the evidence contains.

◇

Compare

Evaluate the evidence against statements, timelines, records, alternative explanations, and other findings.

✓

Interpret

Explain what the evidence supports, what it does not prove, and where uncertainty remains.

FORENSIC STANDARDS

Evidence integrity principles

Investigators must be able to explain how evidence was collected, handled, analyzed, and used. A strong conclusion is transparent about both support and limitations.

ARCHIVE PROTOCOL

Protect the evidence before interpreting it.

Evidence can lose value when its origin, condition, handling, or analytical process cannot be verified. Integrity begins before testing and continues through documentation, comparison, and final reporting.

INTEGRITY VERIFIED
PROTOCOL 01

Document the Source

Record where, when, and by whom the evidence was discovered.

PROTOCOL 02

Preserve Original Condition

Avoid unnecessary handling, editing, cleaning, or alteration.

PROTOCOL 03

Maintain Chain of Custody

Document every transfer, examination, and person responsible for the item.

PROTOCOL 04

Separate Fact From Inference

Clearly distinguish observed information from investigator interpretation.

PROTOCOL 05

Seek Corroboration

Compare each finding with independent records, statements, or testing.

PROTOCOL 06

Report Limitations

Explain uncertainty, missing information, and alternative explanations.
