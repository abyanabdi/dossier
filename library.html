DIGITAL INVESTIGATION ARCHIVE

Evidence Library

A knowledge database designed to help investigators understand digital evidence, cybercrime methods, and forensic investigation techniques.

Investigator Fundamentals

Digital Evidence

Digital evidence is information collected from electronic devices, online platforms, and digital systems that can support an investigation.

Investigators examine emails, files, websites, messages, account activity, and online records to identify patterns and suspicious behaviour.

Used in investigations:

Email analysis, website investigations, digital footprints.

Metadata Analysis

Metadata is hidden information stored inside digital files that provides details about their creation and history.

Investigators may examine timestamps, locations, file ownership, and modification history to determine whether evidence has been altered.

Common evidence:

Documents, images, files, digital records.

Digital Footprints

A digital footprint is the record of online activity created through websites, accounts, and internet use.

Investigators analyze online presence to verify identities, organizations, and suspicious activity.

Investigation clues:

Social media activity, websites, account history.

Cybercrime Investigation

Social Engineering

Social engineering involves manipulating people into revealing information or taking actions that benefit criminals.

Attackers often use trust, urgency, fear, or authority to influence victims.

Common tactics:

Fake job offers, impersonation, urgent requests, and fraudulent communication.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing uses fake emails, messages, or websites to steal information or gain unauthorized access.

Investigators examine sender addresses, language patterns, links, and requests for sensitive information.

Warning signs:

Suspicious domains, unexpected messages, and pressure tactics.

Artificial Intelligence Fraud

AI technology can be used to create realistic fake voices, images, messages, and identities.

Investigators must evaluate whether digital content is authentic or manipulated.

Examples:

Voice cloning, fake profiles, automated scams.

Forensic Investigation Methods

Chain of Custody

Chain of custody documents how evidence is collected, handled, stored, and transferred.

Maintaining evidence integrity ensures that investigators can trust the information.

Evidence Collection

Investigators must carefully identify and preserve digital evidence without changing the original data.

This includes collecting files, messages, metadata, and online records.

Investigator Methodology

Investigations follow a structured process: collect evidence, analyze information, identify patterns, and develop conclusions.

Good investigators rely on evidence rather than assumptions.
