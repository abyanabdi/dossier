Evidence Analysis Modules

LEVEL 1 / BEGINNER

Digital Evidence

Digital evidence includes information collected from computers, websites, online accounts, and electronic communication.

Investigators examine:

Emails, files, messages, websites, account activity, and digital records.

LEVEL 1 / BEGINNER

Email Analysis

Email investigations help identify phishing attempts, fraudulent communication, and suspicious requests.

Investigators examine:

Sender addresses, language patterns, urgency tactics, attachments, and links.

LEVEL 2 / INTERMEDIATE

Metadata Investigation

Metadata provides hidden information about digital files and can reveal how evidence was created.

Investigators examine:

Creation dates, file history, locations, and changes made to digital content.

LEVEL 2 / INTERMEDIATE

Digital Footprints

Online activity creates digital traces that can help investigators verify identities and organizations.

Investigators examine:

Social media activity, websites, usernames, and online history.

Cybercrime Threat Database

Social Engineering

Criminals manipulate human behaviour to gain trust, information, or access.

Common tactics:

Fake job offers, impersonation, urgency, and authority-based deception.

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent messages designed to steal information or convince victims to complete harmful actions.

Warning signs:

Suspicious links, fake domains, unexpected requests, and pressure tactics.

AI Fraud

Artificial intelligence can be used to create fake identities, voices, images, and messages.

Investigators analyze:

Authenticity, manipulation patterns, and source verification.

Digital Forensics Methods

Chain of Custody

The process of documenting how evidence is collected, stored, and handled.

Maintains evidence reliability and prevents tampering.

Evidence Collection

Investigators preserve digital information while ensuring the original evidence remains unchanged.

Examples include emails, files, screenshots, metadata, and online records.

Investigator Methodology

Professional investigations follow a process:

Collect evidence → Analyze patterns → Identify threats → Develop conclusions.

Investigator Checklist

Before closing an investigation, investigators ask:

  • Can the source of the evidence be verified?
  • Is the information consistent across multiple sources?
  • Are there signs of manipulation or deception?
  • What evidence supports the final conclusion?
  • Could the incident have been prevented?
