Investigation Toolkit

SKILL MODULE 001

Email Analysis

Investigators analyze digital communication to determine whether messages are authentic or part of a social engineering attack.

Investigators Check:
  • Sender address and domain
  • Suspicious language patterns
  • Urgency tactics
  • Requests for personal information

Cybercrime Threat Database

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

Social Engineering

Criminals manipulate human behaviour to gain trust, information, or access.

Common Methods:
  • Fake opportunities
  • Impersonation
  • Pressure tactics
  • False authority

THREAT LEVEL: HIGH

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent messages designed to steal information or convince victims to complete harmful actions.

Warning Signs:
  • Fake websites
  • Suspicious links
  • Unexpected requests
  • Unknown senders

THREAT LEVEL: EMERGING

AI Fraud

Artificial intelligence can be used to create realistic fake identities, voices, and content.

Investigators Analyze:
  • Voice cloning
  • Fake images
  • Manipulated media
  • Identity verification

Digital Forensics Methods

Evidence Collection

Investigators collect digital information while preserving its accuracy and reliability.

Examples: Emails, files, screenshots, account records.

Chain of Custody

A documented record showing how evidence was collected, stored, and handled.

This protects evidence integrity during an investigation.

Investigation Process

Professional investigators follow a structured method:

Collect Evidence → Analyze Patterns → Identify Threats → Report Findings

Investigator Checklist

Before closing any investigation, investigators ask:

  • Can the evidence source be verified?
  • Does the information match across multiple sources?
  • Are there signs of manipulation or deception?
  • What evidence supports the conclusion?
  • How could the incident have been prevented?
