CASE FILE 001 / ACTIVE INVESTIGATION

The Internship Scam

A digital investigation into fraudulent recruitment, social engineering tactics, and online deception.

Case Classification

Status Active Investigation
Category Cybercrime
Difficulty Beginner
Evidence Files 3

Case Brief

A university student receives an unexpected internship opportunity from an organization claiming to offer exclusive career placements.

At first glance, the opportunity appears legitimate. The message uses professional language, includes an application process, and creates the impression of a real recruitment opportunity.

After submitting personal information through the application process, the student begins noticing unusual activity across their online accounts.

Investigators have been assigned to determine: Was this a legitimate internship opportunity, or was the student targeted through a carefully designed social engineering attack?

Your task is to analyze the recovered digital evidence, identify suspicious indicators, and determine how the attack was carried out.

Investigation Timeline

Day 1: Victim receives internship offer through email.

Day 2: Victim completes an external application form requesting personal information.

Day 3: Suspicious activity is detected across the victim's online accounts.

Day 4: Investigators begin collecting digital evidence.

Day 5: Evidence analysis reveals possible indicators of fraud.

Investigator Objectives

Your mission is to investigate the internship offer and determine whether it represents a legitimate career opportunity or a cybercrime operation.

Investigators must:

  • Identify suspicious indicators within digital evidence.
  • Analyze communication methods and online activity.
  • Evaluate whether the organization can be verified.
  • Determine what social engineering tactics were used.
  • Recommend prevention strategies for future victims.
