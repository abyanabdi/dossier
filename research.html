RESEARCH STANDARDS

Principles for responsible investigation

Research should improve judgment rather than create false certainty. Investigators must remain transparent about evidence quality, limitations, interpretation, and uncertainty.

DIVISION PROTOCOL

Strong analysis can withstand challenge.

A reliable investigative conclusion is not based on confidence alone. It is built through transparent methods, verifiable evidence, competing explanations, and careful attention to what remains unknown.

RESEARCH REVIEWED
STANDARD 01

Use Reliable Sources

Prioritize credible research, documented methods, and verifiable evidence.

STANDARD 02

Separate Evidence From Assumption

Clearly identify what is known, inferred, disputed, or still unknown.

STANDARD 03

Test Alternative Explanations

Evaluate whether the evidence can reasonably support more than one account.

STANDARD 04

Recognize Bias

Consider how expectations, first impressions, and prior beliefs can influence interpretation.

STANDARD 05

Seek Corroboration

Compare findings with independent records, testing, statements, and observations.

STANDARD 06

Report Limitations

Explain uncertainty, missing information, and the boundaries of the conclusion.
