Purpose of Research

The Dossier Research Division explores the theories, investigative methods, and technologies behind each case simulation. These reports examine how human behaviour, digital systems, physical evidence, and forensic reasoning shape modern investigations.

While Case Files challenge users to solve specific incidents, Research Reports provide the background knowledge needed to understand why crimes occur, how evidence is interpreted, and how investigators build reliable conclusions.

REPORT 001

The Psychology Behind Social Engineering Attacks

Connected Case:
Case File 001: The Internship Scam

Social engineering attacks target human decision-making by using trust, authority, urgency, fear, and opportunity. Instead of relying only on technical weaknesses, offenders manipulate victims into revealing information or completing harmful actions.

Key Concepts:
  • Trust manipulation
  • False authority
  • Urgency and pressure tactics
  • Opportunity-based deception
  • Victim information gathering

Investigator Focus:
Understanding why victims respond to deceptive messages helps investigators identify manipulation strategies and recognize recurring attack patterns.

REPORT 002

Digital Evidence in Modern Investigations

Connected Case:
Case File 001: The Internship Scam

Digital evidence can help investigators reconstruct events, verify identities, trace communications, and determine whether online activity is authentic or suspicious.

Evidence Sources:
  • Emails and message headers
  • Websites and domain records
  • File and device metadata
  • Account access history
  • Online activity records

Investigator Focus:
Evidence must be collected, preserved, verified, and compared with other sources before conclusions are made.

REPORT 003

Artificial Intelligence and Impersonation Fraud

Connected Case:
Case File 002: The Executive Impersonation Incident

Artificial intelligence is creating new investigative challenges through realistic synthetic voices, automated messages, manipulated media, and convincing identity impersonation.

Emerging Threats:
  • Voice cloning
  • Deepfake media
  • Synthetic identities
  • AI-generated communications
  • Automated fraud campaigns

Investigator Focus:
Investigators must verify suspicious communications through independent channels and examine technical indicators before trusting digital content.

REPORT 004

Forensic Toxicology and Suspicious Death Investigation

Connected Case:
Case File 003: Silent Dose

Forensic toxicology examines drugs, chemicals, poisons, and unknown compounds found in biological or physical evidence. Investigators use laboratory findings to determine how a substance entered the body and whether it contributed to injury or death.

Key Concepts:
  • Unknown substance identification
  • Dosage and concentration analysis
  • Contamination prevention
  • Exposure pathways
  • Laboratory evidence interpretation

Investigator Focus:
Laboratory results must be compared with scene evidence, witness accounts, medical history, and timeline information.

REPORT 005

Interview Analysis and Deception Indicators

Connected Case:
Case File 003: Silent Dose

Investigative interviews help establish timelines, clarify relationships, test explanations, and identify contradictions. Behaviour alone does not prove deception, but changes in language, detail, stress, and consistency can guide further investigation.

Investigators Examine:
  • Statement consistency
  • Changes in detail
  • Avoidance and deflection
  • Timeline contradictions
  • Evidence-based reactions

Investigator Focus:
Interview observations should always be supported by independent evidence rather than assumptions about body language alone.

REPORT 006

Reconstructing Events Through Evidence

Connected Cases:
Case Files 001, 002, and 003

Investigators reconstruct events by organizing evidence into a reliable sequence. Communications, account activity, witness statements, physical evidence, and laboratory findings can reveal planning, opportunity, movement, and intent.

Reconstruction Sources:
  • Time-stamped communications
  • Access and activity logs
  • Witness statements
  • Physical evidence
  • Forensic examination results

Investigator Focus:
A strong timeline should explain how each event is supported and identify where evidence remains incomplete.

RESEARCH CATEGORIES

Research Areas

Digital Forensics

The collection, preservation, examination, and interpretation of evidence from computers, mobile devices, online accounts, files, and communication systems.

Cybercrime Behaviour

The study of offender strategies, victim targeting, online deception, social engineering, identity manipulation, and technology-enabled crime.

Artificial Intelligence

The examination of how AI can support investigations while also creating new risks through synthetic media, automation, impersonation, and misinformation.

Forensic Science

The application of scientific methods to physical evidence, chemical substances, biological materials, scene reconstruction, and laboratory analysis.

Investigative Interviewing

The structured process of gathering information, testing explanations, identifying contradictions, and evaluating statements against available evidence.

Criminological Analysis

The study of criminal behaviour, opportunity, motivation, social conditions, victimization, and the broader causes and patterns of crime.

Research Standards

Reliable investigative research should be evidence-based, transparent, and open to alternative explanations.

  • Separate verified facts from assumptions.
  • Use multiple sources whenever possible.
  • Consider the reliability and limitations of each source.
  • Avoid treating one piece of evidence as conclusive.
  • Document how conclusions were reached.
  • Revise conclusions when new evidence becomes available.
