DOSSIER RESEARCH DIVISION

Investigation Reports

Explore the concepts, behaviours, and technologies behind modern digital investigations and cybercrime.

Purpose of Research

The Research Division explores the ideas behind Dossier investigations. These reports examine how technology, human behaviour, and digital evidence shape modern cybercrime investigations.

While Case Files focus on solving specific incidents, Research Reports provide the background knowledge investigators need to understand the bigger picture.

Featured Research Reports

REPORT 001

The Psychology Behind Social Engineering Attacks

Connected Case:
Case File 001: The Internship Scam

Social engineering attacks target human decision making by using trust, urgency, and manipulation instead of only relying on technical attacks.

Key Concepts:
  • Trust manipulation
  • False authority
  • Urgency tactics
  • Information gathering

Investigator Focus:
Understanding why victims respond to deceptive messages helps investigators identify attack patterns.

REPORT 002

Digital Evidence in Modern Investigations

Connected Case:
Case File 001: The Internship Scam

Digital evidence allows investigators to reconstruct events and determine whether online activity is authentic or suspicious.

Evidence Sources:
  • Emails
  • Websites
  • Metadata
  • Online activity records

Investigator Focus:
Evidence must be collected, verified, and analyzed before conclusions are made.

REPORT 003

Artificial Intelligence and Cybercrime

Connected Case:
Case File 002: The AI Voice Incident

Artificial intelligence is creating new challenges for investigators through realistic fake content, automated scams, and identity manipulation.

Emerging Threats:
  • Voice cloning
  • Deepfake media
  • Fake identities
  • AI generated scams

Investigator Focus:
Future investigations will require stronger methods for verifying digital authenticity.
