CASE FILE 002 / EVIDENCE 002-C

Financial Transaction Analysis

Analyze recovered financial records to determine whether a payment was part of legitimate business activity or a coordinated cybercrime operation.

Case Update

Previous investigation stages revealed evidence of a possible executive impersonation attack.

Investigators discovered:

  • A suspicious AI-generated voice recording requesting urgent action.
  • A fraudulent email containing payment instructions.

The next question: Did these communications result in an unauthorized financial transfer?

Investigation Objective

Your role is to examine the recovered transaction record and determine whether financial evidence supports the theory of a coordinated fraud attempt.

Financial investigators do not rely on one suspicious indicator. They analyze patterns, timelines, and connections between multiple evidence sources.

Fraud Pattern Analysis

Unusual Recipient

The company had no previous relationship with the recipient organization.

Financial Pressure

The request created urgency and bypassed normal financial review procedures.

Multiple Attack Methods

The offender combined email impersonation and AI voice technology to increase credibility.

Criminological Analysis: Why Did This Crime Succeed?

The financial transfer was not successful because of technology alone. Investigators must also examine the human and organizational factors that allowed the fraud to occur.

Using Routine Activity Theory, investigators can analyze how the offender, target, and security conditions combined to create an opportunity for crime.

Routine Activity Theory Applied to the Case

Motivated Offender

Evidence Finding: The offender used multiple deception methods, including AI-generated voice impersonation and fraudulent email communication.

Connection: The attacker intentionally created a situation designed to manipulate an employee into approving an unauthorized payment.

Suitable Target

Evidence Finding: The targeted employee had access to financial systems and the ability to process payments.

Connection: The offender selected a target whose workplace role provided access to valuable resources.

Lack of Guardianship

Evidence Finding: The payment request bypassed normal verification procedures.

Connection: Without additional confirmation steps, the offender was able to exploit organizational weaknesses.

Investigator Interpretation

This case demonstrates that cybercrime is not only a technical problem. Successful attacks often combine digital tools with psychological manipulation and organizational vulnerabilities.

The offender exploited technology, workplace trust, and decision-making processes to increase the chance of success.
