CASE FILE 001 · THE INTERNSHIP SCAM

CYBER FRAUD INTAKE

A university student received an internship offer that appeared to come from a respected technology company. After submitting personal information, the student was instructed to deposit a cheque and purchase equipment through a designated vendor.

VICTIM STATEMENT

Maya Chen, 21

“The recruiter knew my program, used the company logo, and interviewed me through chat. The cheque appeared in my account, so I followed the equipment instructions. Two days later, the bank reversed it.”

INITIAL LOSS

$2,480

Personal data exposed: full name, address, phone number, résumé, banking institution, and identification image.

MISSION BRIEF

Determine how the fraud worked

Objectives

Analyze the recruiter email, inspect the fake employer website, trace the digital footprint, and classify the social-engineering attack.

Rules of Evidence

Do not rely on appearance alone. Validate sender identity, domain history, technical metadata, payment instructions, and independent company records.
