THE INTERNSHIP
SCAM

A student lost $2,450 after accepting what appeared to be a legitimate remote internship. Your task is to determine how the offender built trust, trace the connected accounts, and submit an evidence-based finding.

INCIDENT REPORT

Complaint received

Emma Chen, a 21-year-old computer science student, applied for a remote internship after seeing a post on a professional networking platform.

A recruiter calling himself Ryan Cole conducted a text interview, sent a formal offer, and provided a cheque for equipment costs. Emma was ordered to deposit the cheque and immediately purchase a laptop and software from an approved vendor.

Her banking app displayed the deposited amount as available. She sent $2,450 by e-transfer. Two days later, the cheque was reversed as counterfeit. The recruiter, Telegram account, and equipment store disappeared.

Reporting PartyEmma Chen
Date ReportedMarch 14, 2026
Financial Loss$2,450 CAD
ClassificationSuspected Employment Fraud

INVESTIGATION OBJECTIVES

1. Authenticate the recruiter
Inspect the email identity and pressure tactics.
2. Verify the employer
Compare the website with independent records.
3. Trace the infrastructure
Connect the domain, Telegram, vendor, and payment account.
4. Build the case theory
Classify the fraud and recommend victim safeguards.
